First Year Game Project

Scene from a point and click adventure

This was my first big project coding a video game (or at least part of one). It was done using c++ and I created all the assets myself.

The game is a scene from a point and click adventure, where we play as a little witch that becomes the local witch doctor. Patients visit her with their ailments and she has to find out what is wrong with them and how to treat them. From time to time we must set out to gather new ingredients in the local forest, lake or mountains

In this scene the witch is gather ingredients in the mountains where she stumbles upon a giant with a giant splinter in his foot.

I spent a lot of time working on the art assets, creating multiple sketches and deciding on colour and design choices.

I quickly landed on a design for the witch though I tried a couple different colour choices. At first her dress was purple, but I eventually landed on yellow to distinguish her from the blue background.

I had a few designs for the mountain giant as I was not happy with my initial design. I wanted it to look like part of the scenery so I thought about making it look like the top of a mountain with a bit of snow as their hair, but I didn't like that initial design so I made it look more rock like instead.

I created a bunch of assets and added to them as I was programming the game. I decided I wanted the cursor to change depending on what the player is hovering over, so I created some small mouth, eye and hand symbols as well as small versions of the objects the player can collect.

I also created a spritesheet for the player character so it would have a simple walking and talking animation.

Second Year Browser Game Project

Browser game inspired by Papers Please

This is my second major project coding a game, this time using html. Again, I created all the art assets myself.

In this game the player works for an organisation that is planning to send people to mars as part of a new utopia. The player has to decide who gets to go and who doesn't following increasingly detailed instructions.

I got the overall setup working and a couple example files though there is no game play beyond that yet.

I spent some time creating assets for all the apps that the player can click on.

I wanted the files to look like digital versions of the classic police files.

All the apps are created to look like computer apps.

I also created a mascot to act as a tutorial instructor. It is based on a personified planet mars wearing an astronauts helmet.

Second Year Website Group Project

Website for sharing art inspired by deviantart and pinterest

This was my first project programming a website as part of a team. It was done using html and sveltekit and I was responsible for the profile page.

We decided to program a website where people could share their art, inspired by sites like deviantart, artstation or pinterest, but with more focus on community and highlighting each individual artist.

We found that many of the existing websites made it extremely hard for most of their users to get their art seen, so our goal was to fix that. Of course, the scope of the project was very limited, so we only got the skeleton working.

In our three person team we each researched different websites and then came up with a design together.

We landed on a neutral grey purpleish background colour and black and white accents to keep everything very neutral, so the art would be in the focus. The name of our website would be art/srj standing for art surge and being made of of the initials of the three creators (s, r, j)

We each created one of the main pages of the website, between the start page, upload page and profile page. I worked on the profile page and it was fun to figure out the different features, between setting up a grid pattern for the profile and the uploaded images, the enhancing of the images when moused over and the overall layout.


I'm pretty happy with the overall design, though there were a lot of features I didn't get to finish as we ran out of time, like individual pages for each image.

Second Year To-Do App Design

Desiging prototypes for a to do app

This was a smaller project, creating a prototype for an app.

I designed a to do app in which the user can set tasks and earns points when completing them which can be spent to level up a character, defeat monsters and gain treasure.

I mainly worked on the designs for the individual pages and the overall feel of the app.

The purpose of the app is to incentivise the user to do their tasks by gamefying the process and giving them a nice little incentive, but it is still in the users hands to set their tasks and how big or small they are, which will determine how much reward they get.
